1. What Are Cookies

When you visit our website at jp-management-global.us (the "Website"), operated by JP Management, Lubanska 50/10, 59-900 Zgorzelec, Poland, we use various technologies to collect and store information about your visit. This Cookies Policy explains what these technologies are, why we use them, and your rights to control their use.

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer, smartphone, or other device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, to provide a better user experience, and to supply information to the operators of the website. Cookies can be "first-party cookies" (set by us directly) or "third-party cookies" (set by a domain other than ours, such as an analytics or advertising provider).

Tracking pixels (also known as web beacons or pixel tags) are tiny, transparent image files embedded in web pages or emails. When a page containing a pixel is loaded, the pixel sends a request to a remote server, which records information such as your IP address, browser type, and the time the page was accessed. We use tracking pixels from Meta (Facebook) and other providers to measure the effectiveness of our advertising campaigns and understand user behaviour.

Local storage (localStorage) is a web browser feature that allows websites to store data directly in your browser. Unlike cookies, localStorage data is not automatically sent to the server with each request. We use localStorage to remember your language preference (via i18next) so that the Website is displayed in your chosen language on subsequent visits.

JavaScript tags and scripts are code snippets loaded by your browser that can collect information about your interaction with the Website, such as pages visited, links clicked, and scrolling behaviour. Tag management systems like Google Tag Manager serve as containers that load and manage these scripts on our behalf.

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies and similar technologies on our Website for the following general purposes:

Functionality: Certain cookies and storage mechanisms are strictly necessary for the Website to function correctly. These include session cookies that maintain the state of your browsing session, localStorage entries that remember your preferred language, and security tokens used by Google reCAPTCHA Enterprise to protect our forms from automated abuse and bot traffic. Without these technologies, key features of the Website would not operate as intended.

Analytics: We use analytics cookies and scripts to understand how visitors interact with our Website. This includes information such as which pages are visited most frequently, how long visitors spend on each page, where visitors come from, and how they navigate through the site. We use Matomo Analytics for general website usage analysis, Hotjar for heatmaps and session recordings that show us how users visually interact with pages, and Ahrefs for SEO performance analytics. This data helps us improve the Website's structure, content, and overall user experience.

Marketing: Marketing cookies and pixels are used to track visitors across websites and to display advertisements that are relevant and engaging for the individual user. We use the Meta (Facebook) Pixel to measure the effectiveness of our advertising campaigns on Facebook and Instagram, to build custom audiences for targeted advertising, and to track conversions (such as form submissions) that result from our ads. Google Tag Manager is used as a container to manage the deployment of these marketing tags. These cookies are only set with your prior consent.

5. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

In addition to using our consent management tool (see Section 6 below), you can control and manage cookies through your web browser settings. Most browsers allow you to view, delete, and block cookies from websites. Please note that if you delete or block cookies, some features of our Website may not function as intended, and your user experience may be affected.

Below are instructions for managing cookies in the most commonly used browsers:

Google Chrome: Click the three-dot menu in the upper-right corner, then select "Settings." Navigate to "Privacy and security," then click "Cookies and other site data." From here you can choose to block third-party cookies, block all cookies, or clear existing cookies. You can also add specific sites to an allow or block list. For more information, visit: https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/95647

Mozilla Firefox: Click the hamburger menu (three horizontal lines) in the upper-right corner, then select "Settings." Navigate to "Privacy & Security." Under "Enhanced Tracking Protection," you can choose Standard, Strict, or Custom settings. The Custom option allows you to select specifically which types of cookies and trackers to block. For more information, visit: https://support.mozilla.org/en-US/kb/cookies-information-websites-store-on-your-computer

Apple Safari: Open Safari and go to "Safari" in the menu bar, then select "Settings" (or "Preferences" on older versions). Click the "Privacy" tab. Here you can choose to "Prevent cross-site tracking" and manage website data. You can also remove stored cookies and data for individual or all websites. For more information, visit: https://support.apple.com/guide/safari/manage-cookies-sfri11471/mac

Microsoft Edge: Click the three-dot menu in the upper-right corner, then select "Settings." Navigate to "Cookies and site permissions," then click "Manage and delete cookies and site data." You can choose to block third-party cookies, block all cookies, or clear cookies when you close the browser. For more information, visit: https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/microsoft-edge/manage-cookies-in-microsoft-edge

To clear localStorage data (such as the language preference), you can use your browser's developer tools. In most browsers, press F12 to open developer tools, navigate to the "Application" or "Storage" tab, and locate "Local Storage" in the sidebar. From there, you can view and delete individual entries or clear all localStorage data for our Website.

In accordance with Section 25(1) TDDDG and Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR, we obtain your explicit, informed consent before placing any cookies or similar technologies on your device that are not strictly necessary for the provision of our service. Your right to withdraw consent: You have the right to withdraw your consent at any time. Withdrawing consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out on the basis of consent before its withdrawal. To withdraw or modify your cookie preferences, you can use our cookie consent tool, which is accessible at any time via the cookie settings link in the footer of our Website. When you withdraw consent, the relevant cookies will no longer be set during future visits. To remove cookies that have already been placed on your device, please follow the browser-specific instructions in Section 5 above.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority if you believe your data protection rights have been infringed. The competent supervisory authority for data protection in Poland is the Prezes Urzedu Ochrony Danych Osobowych (PUODO), ul. Stawki 2, 00-193 Warsaw, Poland.

For any questions or concerns regarding cookies and data protection, please contact us at: info@jp-management.com
